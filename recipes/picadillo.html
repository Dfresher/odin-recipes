Picadillo

Description:

Picadillo is a savory and flavorful Venezuelan dish made with ground meat, typically beef or a combination of beef and pork.

It's seasoned with a mix of spices, tomatoes, and aromatics, creating a hearty and satisfying meal. This versatile dish can be served with rice, arepas, or as a filling for empanadas.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef (or a combination of beef and pork)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup green olives, sliced
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons capers
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup water or beef broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. Sauté Aromatics:
    • Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
    • Add chopped onions and minced garlic. Sauté until onions are translucent.
  2. Brown Ground Meat:
    • Add the ground beef to the skillet. Break it apart with a spoon and cook until browned.
  3. Add Bell Peppers and Tomatoes:
    • Incorporate diced red and green bell peppers along with chopped tomatoes. Cook until the vegetables are softened.
  4. Season the Picadillo:
    • Stir in tomato paste, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well to coat the meat and vegetables with the spices.
  5. Add Raisins, Olives, and Capers:
    • Optionally, add raisins for a touch of sweetness, sliced green olives for brininess, and capers for a burst of flavor.
  6. Simmer:
    • Pour in water or beef broth. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let the picadillo simmer for about 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  7. Adjust Seasoning:
    • Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You can add more salt, pepper, or spices according to your preference.
  8. Serve:
    • Serve the picadillo hot over rice, with arepas, or use it as a filling for empanadas.
